What inspires us?

Trees fight climate change

Wish you could do more than recycling and reducing your carbon footprint to combat climate change? Trees have you covered. Through photosynthesis, trees absorb harmful carbon dioxide, removing and storing the carbon and releasing oxygen back into the air.

Trees Cleans The Air

Trees don’t just absorb CO2. They
also absorb odors and pollutants like nitrogen oxides, ammonia, sulfur dioxide and ozone.
It’s estimated that one tree can absorb nearly 10 pounds of polluted air each year and release 260 pounds of oxygen.

Prevent soil erosion

During heavy rains, water runoff finds its way to streams,
lakes and wetlands, creating the potential for flooding.
It also picks up and carries pollutants along the way.
The EPA Sierra Leone recognized the importance of trees. Leaf canopies help buffer the falling rain and their roots hold the soil in place, encouraging the water to seep into the ground rather than run off.

Home Value

Studies of comparable homes with
and without trees show that, if you have trees in your yard, your home’s value increases by up to 15 percent. It’s all about curb appeal, and trees make your home and yard more beautiful.

Economy

Trees conserve energy in dry season, providing shade from the hot sun.
With trees standing between you and the elements, you’ll spend less on your energy bill to heat and cool your home.
